Theorem 3.3.7. Products of invertible matrices.

Let \(A,B\) be \(n\times n\) matrices. If \(A\) and \(B\) are both invertible, then so is their product \(AB\text{.}\) Using logical notation:
\begin{equation} A\text{ and } B \text{ invertible } \implies AB \text{ invertible }\text{.}\tag{3.3.2} \end{equation}
In fact when this is the case we have
\begin{equation} (AB)^{-1}=B^{-1}A^{-1}\text{.}\tag{3.3.3} \end{equation}
