Example 4.4.7. Dimension of symmetric matrices.

Let \(W\subseteq M_{22}\) be the space of symmetric \(2\times 2\) matrices. Compute \(\dim W\) using Procedure 4.4.14. You may take for granted that \(W\) is a subspace.
Solution.
We saw in Example 4.3.13 that \(B=\{A_1, A_2, A_3\}\) is a basis of \(W\text{,}\) where
\begin{equation*} A_1= \begin{bmatrix} 1\amp 0\\ 0\amp 0 \end{bmatrix}, A_2=\begin{bmatrix} 0\amp 1\\ 1\amp 0 \end{bmatrix} , A_3=\begin{bmatrix} 0\amp 0\\ 0\amp 1 \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}
We conclude that \(\dim W=\abs{B}=3\text{.}\)
