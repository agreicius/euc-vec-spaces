Procedure 5.5.16. Computing the standard matrix using change of basis.

Let \(T\colon \R^n\rightarrow \R^n\) be a linear transformation, and let \(A\) be its standard matrix. To compute \(A\) using the change of basis formula (5.5.5), proceed as follows.
  1. Find a convenient basis \(B'\) for which the action of \(T\) is easily understood.
  2. Compute \(A'=[T]_{B'}\text{.}\)
  3. Let \(B\) be the standard basis of \(\R^n\text{.}\) Recall that \(A=[T]_B\text{.}\) Now compute \(A\) using the change of basis formula as
    \begin{equation*} A=\underset{B'\rightarrow B}{P}\, A'\, \underset{B\rightarrow B'}{P}\text{.} \end{equation*}
