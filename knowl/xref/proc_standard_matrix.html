Procedure 5.1.29. Standard matrix.

Let \(T\colon \R^n\rightarrow \R^m\) be a linear transformation and let \(B=\{\bolde_1,\bolde_2,\dots, \bolde_n\}\) be the standard basis of \(\R^n\text{.}\) The standard matrix of \(T\) is the matrix whose \(j\)-th column is the result of applying \(T\) to \(\bolde_j\text{:}\) i.e.,
\begin{equation} A=\begin{bmatrix}\vert\amp \vert\amp \amp \vert \\ T(\bolde_1)\amp T(\bolde_2)\amp \cdots \amp T(\bolde_n)\\ \vert\amp \vert\amp \amp \vert \end{bmatrix}\text{.}\tag{5.1.2} \end{equation}
