Theorem 5.2.21.

Let \(V\) be a vector space of finite dimension \(n\text{.}\)
  1. A vector space \(W\) is isomorphic to \(V\) if and only if \(\dim V=\dim W\text{.}\)
  2. Assume \(W\) is a vector space satisfying \(\dim V=\dim W=n\text{,}\) and let \(T\colon V\rightarrow W\) be a linear transformation. The following are equivalent.
    1. \(T\colon V\rightarrow W\) is an isomorphism.
    2. \(\NS T=\{\boldzero_V\}\) (i.e., \(T\) is injective).
    3. \(\im T=W\) (i.e., \(T\) is surjective).
