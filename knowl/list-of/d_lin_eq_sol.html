Definition 2.1.3. Solutions to linear equations.

A solution to a linear equation
\begin{equation*} a_1x_1+a_2x_2+\cdots a_nx_n=b \end{equation*}
is an \(n\)-tuple \((s_1,s_2,\dots, s_n)\) of real numbers for which the variable assignment \(x_1=s_1, x_2=s_2,\dots, x_n=s_n\) makes the equation true: i.e., the tuple \((s_1,s_2,\dots, s_n)\) satisfies
\begin{equation*} a_1s_1+a_2s_2+\cdots a_ns_n=b\text{.} \end{equation*}
We say \((s_1,\dots ,s_n)\) solves the equation in this case.
