Exercise 3.

Consider the functions \(f(x) = \sqrt[7]{\frac{x}{4}} + 8\text{,}\) \(g(x) = -8 + 4 x^{7}\text{,}\) \(h(x) = \sqrt[7]{\frac{x}{4}} - 8\text{,}\) \(p(x) = \sqrt[7]{\frac{x - 8}{4}}\text{,}\) \(q(x) = 8 + 4 x^{7}\text{,}\) and \(r(x) = \sqrt[7]{\frac{x + 8}{4}}\text{.}\)
Which of these functions are inverses of each other?
  • \(p(x)\) and \(q(x)\)
  • \(g(x)\) and \(p(x)\)
  • \(f(x)\) and \(q(x)\)
  • \(h(x)\) and \(q(x)\)
  • \(g(x)\) and \(h(x)\)
  • \(q(x)\) and \(r(x)\)
  • \(g(x)\) and \(r(x)\)
  • \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\)
  • No pairs of these functions are inverses of each other.
Solution.
SOLUTION One way we can check which functions are inverses is to make sure they satisfy the identities \(f \left( f^{-1}(x) \right)=x\) and \(f^{-1} \left( f(x) \right)=x\text{.}\) This will show that \(p(x)\) and \(q(x)\) and \(g(x)\) and \(r(x)\) are inverses of each other, while the other pairs of functions are not. Letâ€™s consider \(p(x)\) and \(q(x)\text{.}\) The composition
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} p\left(q(x)\right) \amp = 8 + 4 \left(\sqrt[7]{ \frac{x - 8}{4}}\right)^{7} \\ \amp = 8 + 4 \left( \frac{x - 8}{4} \right) \\ \amp = 8 + (x-8) = x. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
Also,
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} q\left(p(x)\right) \amp = \sqrt[7]{\frac{\left( 8+4 x^{7} \right)-8}{4}} \\ \amp = \sqrt[7]{x^{7}} = x. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
Thus these are inverses of one another. We can similarly show that \(g(x)\) and \(r(x)\) are inverses of each other. However, the remaining pairs are not inverses. For example, if we consider \(q(x)\) and \(r(x)\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} q\left(r(x)\right) \amp = 8 + 4 \left( \sqrt[7]{ \frac{x + 8}{4}} \right)^{7}\\ \amp = 8 + (x+8) = x + 16 \ne x. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
Thus these functions are not inverses of each other.
