Example 5.4.10. Computing with matrix representations.

The function
\begin{align*} F\colon M_{22} \amp \rightarrow M_{22}\\ A \amp \mapsto A^T-A \end{align*}
is linear.
  1. Compute \(A=[T]_{B}\text{,}\) where \(B=(E_{11},E_{12},E_{21},E_{22})\) is the standard basis of \(M_{22}\text{.}\)
  2. Compute bases of \(\NS A\) and \(\CS A\text{.}\)
  3. “Lift” these bases to bases of \(\NS T\) and \(\im T\text{,}\) using the coordinate vector isomorphism \([\phantom{\boldv}]_B\text{.}\)
Solution.
First compute
\begin{align*} F(E_{11}) \amp =\begin{amatrix}[rr]0\amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 0\end{amatrix}\\ F(E_{12}) \amp =\begin{amatrix}[rr]0\amp -1 \\ 1 \amp 0\end{amatrix}\\ F(E_{21}) \amp =\begin{amatrix}[rr]0\amp 1 \\ -1 \amp 0\end{amatrix}\\ F(E_{22}) \amp =\begin{amatrix}[rr]0 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 0\end{amatrix} \end{align*}
