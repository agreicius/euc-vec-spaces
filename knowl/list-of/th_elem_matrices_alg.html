Procedure 3.4.11. Product of elementary matrices algorithm.

Let \(A\) be an \(n\times n\) matrix. To (potentially) write \(A\) as a product of elementary matrices, proceed as follows.
  1. Attempt to row reduce \(A\) to the identity, keeping track of your sequence of row operations in the form of elementary matrices.
  2. If you are able to row reduce \(A\) to \(I_n\) with a sequence of row operatons corresponding to the elementary matrices \(E_1, E_2, \dots, E_r\text{,}\) then
    \begin{equation*} A=E_1^{-1}E_2^{-1}\cdots E_{r}^{-1}. \end{equation*}
    Since the inverse of an elementary matrix is elementary, we have written \(A\) as a product of elementary matrices.
