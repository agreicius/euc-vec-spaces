Example 6.1.9. Scaling transformations.

Let \(V\) be a vector space, let \(c\in \R\) be a fixed scalar, and let \(T\colon V\rightarrow V\) be the scaling transformation defined as \(T(\boldv)=c\boldv\) for all \(\boldv\in V\text{.}\) Find all eigenvectors and eigenvalues of \(T\text{.}\)
Solution.
Since \(T(\boldv)=c\boldv\) for all \(\boldv\text{,}\) we see that all nonzero vectors of \(V\) are eigenvectors with eigenvalue \(c\text{.}\) It follows that \(c\) is the only eigenvalue of \(T\text{.}\)
