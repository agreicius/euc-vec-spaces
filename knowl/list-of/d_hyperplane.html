Definition 2.1.4. Hyperplane.

A linear equation
\begin{equation} a_1x_1+a_2x_2+\cdots a_nx_n=b\tag{2.1.2} \end{equation}
is nontrivial if \(a_i\ne 0\) for some \(1\leq i\leq n\text{.}\) Given a nontrivial equation (2.1.2), the set of solutions
\begin{equation*} S=\{(s_1,s_2,\dots, s_n)\in \R^n\colon a_1s_1+\cdots+a_ns_n=b \} \end{equation*}
is called a hyperplane in \(\R^n\text{.}\) We have the two following special cases, corresponding to \(n=2\) and \(n=3\text{:}\)
  • A hyperplane in \(\R^2\) is the set of solutions to a nontrivial equation \(ax+by=c\text{,}\) and is called a line.
  • A hyperplane in \(\R^3\) is the set of solutions to a nontrivial equation \(ax+by+cz=d\text{,}\) and is called a plane.
