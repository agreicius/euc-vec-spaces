Exercise 19.
Let \(\lambda\) be an eigenvalue of the matrix \(A\text{.}\)
  1. Prove by induction: \(\lambda^k\) is an eigenvalue of \(A^k\) for any positive integer \(k\text{.}\)
  2. Assume that \(A\) is invertible. Prove: \(\lambda^k\) is an eigenvalue of \(A^k\) for any integer \(k\text{.}\)
