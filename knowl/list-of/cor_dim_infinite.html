Corollary 4.4.12. Infinite dimensional spaces.

Let \(V\) be a vector space. If for all integers \(n\) we can find a linearly independent subset \(S\subseteq V\) with \(\abs{S}> n\text{,}\) then \(V\) is infinite dimensional. In other words, if \(V\) contains linearly independent subsets of arbitrarily large cardinality, then \(\dim V=\infty\text{.}\)
