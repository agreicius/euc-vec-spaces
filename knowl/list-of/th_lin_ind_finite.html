Theorem 4.2.12. Linear independence of finite sets.

Let \(V\) be a vector space, and let \(S=\{\boldv_1,\boldv_2,\dots, \boldv_n\}\subseteq V\text{,}\) where the \(\boldv_i\) are distinct. The following are equivalent.
  1. \(S\) is linearly independent.
  2. For all \(c_1,c_2,\dots, c_n\in \R\text{,}\)
    \begin{equation} c_1\boldv_1+c_2\boldv_2+\cdots +c_n\boldv_n=\boldzero \implies c_1=c_2=\cdots =c_n=0\text{.}\tag{4.2.1} \end{equation}
