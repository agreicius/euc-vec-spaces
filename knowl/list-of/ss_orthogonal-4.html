Example 7.2.3.

Show that the set \(S=\{\boldx_1=(1,1,1),\boldx_2=(1,2,-3), \boldx_3=(-5,4,1)\}\) is orthogonal with respect to the dot product. Explain why it follows that \(S\) is a basis of \(\R^3\text{.}\)
Solution.
A simple computation shows \(\boldx_i\cdot \boldx_j=0\) for all \(1\leq i\ne j\leq 3\text{,}\) showing that \(S\) is orthogonal. Theorem 7.2.2 implies \(S\) is linearly independent. Since \(\val{S}=\dim \R^3=3\text{,}\) it follows from Corollary 4.4.16 that \(S\) is a basis.
