Corollary 5.2.14.

Let \(T\colon V\rightarrow W\) be a linear transformation, and suppose \(V\) is finite dimensional.
  1. If \(\dim V > \dim W\text{,}\) then \(T\) is not injective.
  2. If \(\dim V < \dim W\text{,}\) then \(T\) is not surjective.
  3. The following statements are equivalent.
    1. \(T\) is bijective.
    2. \(\dim V=\dim W\) and \(\NS T=\{\boldzero\}\text{.}\)
    3. \(\dim V=\dim W\) and \(\im T=\dim W\text{.}\)
