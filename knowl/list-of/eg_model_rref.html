Example 2.2.14. Reduced row echelon form.

We continue our work in Example 2.2.12 to reach a matrix in reduced row echelon form.
\begin{align*} \begin{bmatrix}\boxed{1}\amp 2\amp -5\amp 3\amp 6\amp 14\\ 0\amp 0\amp \boxed{1}\amp 0\amp -7/2\amp -6\\ 0\amp 0\amp 0\amp 0\amp \boxed{1}\amp 2 \end{bmatrix} \amp \xrightarrow[\hspace{35pt}]{r_2+7/2r_3}\amp \begin{bmatrix}\boxed{1}\amp 2\amp -5\amp 3\amp 6\amp 14\\ 0\amp 0\amp \boxed{1}\amp 0\amp 0\amp 1\\ 0\amp 0\amp 0\amp 0\amp \boxed{1}\amp 2 \end{bmatrix}\\ \amp \xrightarrow[\hspace{35pt}]{r_1-6r_3}\amp \begin{bmatrix}\boxed{1}\amp 2\amp -5\amp 3\amp 0\amp 2\\ 0\amp 0\amp \boxed{1}\amp 0\amp 0\amp 1\\ 0\amp 0\amp 0\amp 0\amp \boxed{1}\amp 2 \end{bmatrix}\\ \amp \xrightarrow[\hspace{35pt}]{r_1+5r_2}\amp \begin{bmatrix}\boxed{1}\amp 2\amp 0\amp 3\amp 0\amp 7\\ 0\amp 0\amp \boxed{1}\amp 0\amp 0\amp 1\\ 0\amp 0\amp 0\amp 0\amp \boxed{1}\amp 2 \end{bmatrix} \text{.} \end{align*}
Notice that the resulting system is slightly simpler than the system in row echelon form of Example 2.2.12:
\begin{equation*} \begin{linsys}{5} x_1\amp+\amp 2x_2\amp \amp \amp+\amp 3x_4\amp +\amp 6x_5\amp =\amp 7\\ \amp \amp \amp \amp x_3\amp \amp \amp \amp \amp =\amp 1\\ \amp \amp \amp \amp \amp \amp \amp \amp x_5\amp =\amp 2 \end{linsys}\text{.} \end{equation*}
