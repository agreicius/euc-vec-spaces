Example 4.4.11. Infinite dimensional space.

Let \(\R^\infty\) be the vector space of infinite sequences of real numbers. (See Example 1.1.11.) We will show that \(\R^\infty\) has not finite basis, and hence that \(\dim \R^\infty=\infty\text{.}\)
Suppose by contradiction that \(\R^\infty\) had a finite basis of cardinality \(n\text{.}\) It would then follow by Theorem 4.4.2, that any set of \(n+1\) elements of \(\R^\infty\) would be linearly independent. Consider the set
\begin{equation*} S=\{\bolds_1,\bolds_2,\dots, \bolds_{n+1}\}\text{,} \end{equation*}
where for each \(1\leq i\leq n\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} \bolds_i=(0,\dots, 0,\underset{i-\text{th entry}}{\underbrace{1}},0,\dots) \end{equation*}
is the sequence whose \(i\)-th term is equal to one, and whose other terms are all equal to zero.
