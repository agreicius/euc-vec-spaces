Exercise 15. Two-dimensional subspaces of \(\R^3\).

Prove that the 2-dimensional subspaces of \(\R^3\) are precisely the planes that pass through the origin. In other words, show (a) that any plane \(\mathcal{P}\subseteq\R^3\) passing through the origin is a 2-dimensional subspace, and conversely, (b) that any 2-dimensional subspace \(W\subseteq \R^3\) is a plane passing through the origin.
Hint.
For (b), begin with a basis of \(B=\{(a,b,c), (d,e,f)\}\) of \(W\text{,}\) and use the cross product to find a normal vector \(\boldn\) that defines \(W\) as a plane.
