(a) Find the distance from the point \(P_0 = (4,6,3)\) to the plane \(-4 x + 2 y + 3 z = -1\text{.}\) Use sqrt() to enter square roots.

(b) Find the equation of the plane that passes through the points \(P = (-4,2,-1)\text{,}\) \(Q = (1,5,4)\text{,}\) and \(R = (-3,-1,-1)\text{.}\) Write your answer in terms of the variables \(x\text{,}\) \(y\text{,}\) \(z\text{.}\)
Answer:
Answer 1.
\(1.11417\)
Answer 2.
\(18z-\left(15x+5y\right) = 32\)
Solution.
Solution: (a) The idea is to find a convenient point lying in the given plane. For example, let us pick \(P_1 = (1,0,1)\text{,}\) and let \(\mathbf{v}\) be the vector from \(P_1\) to \(P_0\text{.}\) Then the distance from \(P_0\) to the plane is the absolute value of the scalar projection of \(\mathbf{v}\) onto the normal vector
\begin{equation*} \mathbf{N} = \left[ \begin{array}{c} -4 \amp 2 \amp 3 \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}
So, we get that the distance is \(\frac{6}{\sqrt{29}}\text{.}\)
(b) To find an equation of the plane in question, we can work with the point \(P\) and the normal vector \(\mathbf{N}\) given by the cross product of the vector from \(P\) to \(Q\text{,}\) and the vector from \(P\) to \(R\text{.}\) It turns out that
\begin{equation*} \mathbf{N} = \left[ \begin{array}{c} -15 \amp -5 \amp 18 \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}
So, an equation of the plane is \(-15 x - 5 y+18 z= 32\text{.}\)
