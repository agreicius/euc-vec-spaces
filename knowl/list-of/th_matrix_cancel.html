Corollary 3.2.10. Failure of multiplicative cancellation.

  1. Suppose matrices \(A, B, C\) satisfy \(AB=AC\) and \(A\ne \boldzero\text{.}\) We cannot conclude that \(B=C\text{.}\) In logical notation:
    \begin{equation} AB=AC \;\not\!\!\!\!\implies B=C\tag{3.2.2} \end{equation}
  2. Suppose matrices \(B, C, D\) satisfy \(BD=CD\) and \(D\ne \boldzero\text{.}\) We cannot conclude that \(B=C\text{.}\) In logical notation:
    \begin{equation} BD=CD \;\not\!\!\!\!\implies B=C\tag{3.2.3} \end{equation}
